Signs You May Need Squirrel Removal Services

Identifying a squirrel problem early is key to preventing extensive damage and costly repairs. While seeing a squirrel in your yard is common, certain signs indicate they have taken up residence in your home or business. Being aware of these indicators can help you take prompt action and contact professional squirrel removal services in Kankakee, IL.

Scratching and Gnawing Noises

One of the most common signs of a squirrel infestation in your home is the presence of unusual noises, particularly in the attic, walls, or ceiling. Squirrels are active during the day, so you are likely to hear scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds throughout the morning and afternoon. These noises are often indicative of squirrels moving around, building nests, or chewing on materials. The sounds might be quiet scratching or more noticeable thumping and chattering. Don't dismiss these noises as simply settling sounds; they can signal a growing problem that requires attention. The constant gnawing is a natural behavior for rodents to keep their teeth filed down, and unfortunately, building materials serve this purpose quite well, leading to significant damage over time.

Damage to Your Property

Squirrels have incredibly strong teeth and a natural instinct to chew. This behavior can lead to significant damage to various parts of your property. Look for evidence of gnawing on wood trim, fascia boards, soffits, or even electrical wiring. Damaged wires pose a serious fire hazard and should be addressed immediately. You might also find torn insulation in your attic or walls as squirrels gather materials for nesting. They may also damage vent covers, screens, and other exterior elements to gain access. Inspect your roof and eaves carefully for any signs of chewing or broken components.

Visible Entry Points

Squirrels need a way to get in and out of your building. Keep an eye out for visible holes, gaps, or openings on the exterior of your property. These can be anywhere from the roofline down to the foundation. Common entry points include damaged roof shingles, uncapped chimneys, gaps around vents, and openings in siding or fascia boards. Sometimes, these entry points are small and difficult to spot without a close inspection. If you notice any new holes or damage near potential access areas, it's a strong indicator that squirrels may be using them to enter your home.

Presence of Droppings or Nesting Materials

Finding squirrel droppings or nesting materials inside your attic, chimney, or other areas can be a clear sign of an infestation. Squirrel droppings are typically small, pellet-shaped, and may be found in concentrated areas. They might also be mixed with nesting materials such as leaves, twigs, insulation, or shredded fabric. These signs indicate that squirrels are using your property as a shelter and breeding ground. The presence of droppings also poses a health risk, as they can carry bacteria and parasites. Proper cleanup and sanitation are essential after removal.

Unusual Odors

A persistent, unpleasant odor inside your home, especially in the attic or walls, can also signal a squirrel problem. This odor may be caused by squirrel urine, droppings, or even decomposing carcasses. As the infestation grows, the odor is likely to become more pronounced. These smells can also attract other pests, exacerbating the problem. If you detect a strange and lingering odor that you cannot explain, it's worth investigating whether wildlife is responsible.

Aggressive or Fearless Behavior

While generally wary of humans, squirrels that have become accustomed to living in close proximity to people or are protecting their young may exhibit more aggressive or fearless behavior. They might approach you in your yard or show little fear if you encounter them inside your home. While not always a sign of an infestation, it can indicate that they have established a presence and feel comfortable in the area, possibly due to readily available food sources or shelter.

Damaged Bird Feeders or Gardens

If you have bird feeders or a garden, you might notice increased squirrel activity around these areas. They are notorious for raiding bird feeders and digging up bulbs or vegetables in gardens. While this doesn't necessarily mean they are in your home, it indicates a local squirrel population that could eventually seek shelter inside. Taking steps to deter squirrels from these outdoor areas can sometimes help prevent them from exploring your house as a potential habitat.

Seeing Squirrels Entering or Exiting Your Home

The most definitive sign of a squirrel problem is actually seeing them entering or exiting specific points on your home. Observe your property during the day to see if you notice any squirrels repeatedly using the same access point. They are most active in the morning and late afternoon. If you visualize this behavior, it confirms they are using your home for shelter or nesting. This observation can be invaluable in helping professionals like Kankakee Pest Pros pinpoint the exact location of the problem.

If you notice any of these signs, it's important to act quickly. Squirrels can reproduce rapidly, and a small problem can quickly escalate into a significant infestation. Early intervention by professional squirrel removal experts in Kankakee, IL, can save you from considerable damage and the stress of a prolonged wildlife issue. Common Problems Caused by Squirrels

Squirrels might look cute and harmless, but their presence in and around your home can lead to a variety of serious and costly problems. Understanding the potential damage they can inflict can highlight the importance of addressing an infestation promptly with professional squirrel removal in Kankakee, IL.

Electrical Hazards and Fire Risks

One of the most dangerous issues squirrels can cause is chewing on electrical wiring. Their constant need to gnaw means that wiring inside your walls or attic is a prime target. Damaged insulation on electrical wires can lead to short circuits, power outages, and, most alarmingly, a significant fire hazard. This type of damage is often hidden within walls or attics and may not be immediately visible, making professional inspection and intervention even more critical. The cost of repairing electrical damagecaused by squirrels can be substantial, not to mention the potential for devastating fires. Protecting your home and family from this risk is paramount, and professional removal and repair are the safest course of action.

Damage to Insulation

Squirrels frequently use insulation as nesting material. They will chew and tear at fiberglass or cellulose insulation in your attic or walls, creating tunnels and nests. This not only reduces the effectiveness of your insulation, leading to higher energy bills, but it also leaves behind droppings and urine contaminated areas. Damaged insulation compromises the thermal efficiency of your home, making it harder to regulate indoor temperature and increasing heating and cooling costs. Furthermore, the presence of squirrel waste in insulation creates unsanitary conditions and can attract other pests. Professional cleanup and replacement of damaged insulation are often necessary after squirrel removal.

Structural Damage

Beyond insulation, squirrels can also damage the structural components of your home. They may gnaw on wooden beams, rafters, siding, trim, and fascia boards to create or enlarge entry points. Over time, this damage can weaken structural elements and compromise the integrity of your home. Repairing this type of damage can be extensive and expensive, especially if it has gone unnoticed for a long period. Preventing squirrels from accessing your property is the best way to avoid this type of structural harm.

Contamination and Health Risks

Squirrel droppings and urine can accumulate in infested areas, creating unsanitary conditions. These waste products can contain harmful bacteria, parasites, and viruses that can pose health risks to your family and pets. Additionally, squirrels can carry fleas, ticks, and mites that can infest your home and potentially transmit diseases. The presence of nesting materials can also attract insects and other pests. Cleaning up contaminated areas requires caution and proper protective gear. Professional cleanup services are often recommended to ensure thorough sanitation and removal of all hazardous materials.

Damage to Plumbing and HVAC Systems

In some cases, squirrels may chew on pipes, vents, or ductwork within your walls or attic. Damaged pipes can lead to leaks and water damage, while damaged ductwork can reduce the efficiency of your heating and cooling systems and potentially spread contaminants throughout your home. Repairing these systems can be complicated and require the expertise of multiple tradespeople. Preventing squirrel access to these vulnerable areas is crucial to avoiding costly repairs.

Noise and Disruption

Beyond the physical damage, the constant noise and activity of squirrels in your home can be incredibly disruptive and stressful. The sounds of scratching, scurrying, and chattering can be particularly bothersome at night, interfering with sleep and overall peace of mind. Living with a squirrel infestation can be a source of significant anxiety and discomfort for homeowners. Removing the source of the noise is essential for restoring tranquility to your home.

Damage to Gardens and Landscaping

While not directly inside your home, squirrels can also cause considerable damage to your outdoor property. They are notorious for digging up flower bulbs, eating fruits and vegetables from gardens, and damaging trees by stripping bark or gnawing on branches. While this type of damage may seem less critical than indoor problems, it can still be a source of frustration and financial loss for homeowners who take pride in their landscaping. Implementing outdoor deterrence methods can sometimes help mitigate these issues.

Attracting Other Pests

Squirrel nests and waste can attract a variety of other pests, including insects and rodents. The presence of these secondary infestations can further complicate the problem and potentially introduce additional health risks and damage. Dealing with multiple types of pests simultaneously adds another layer of complexity to pest control efforts. Addressing the initial squirrel problem thoroughly is key to preventing these secondary infestations.

Choosing the Right Time for Squirrel Removal

While squirrel removal is a service that can be needed at any time of year, certain periods present unique challenges and considerations. Understanding the seasonal behavior of squirrels can help you determine the best time to address an infestation and what to expect during the removal process. At Kankakee Pest Pros, we are equipped to handle squirrel removal services throughout the year in Kankakee, IL, adjusting our methods based on the season.

Spring and Summer: Peak Activity Seasons

Spring and summer are typically the busiest seasons for wildlife control professionals dealing with squirrels. This is the primary breeding season for squirrels, and females often seek safe and sheltered locations, such as attics or walls, to build nests and raise their young. During this time, you are more likely to notice increased squirrel activity around your home as they gather nesting materials and forage for food. The presence of young squirrels, or pups, adds a layer of complexity to the removal process. While we always strive for humane removal, relocating young animals requires extra care and consideration to ensure their survival. Removing adult squirrels without addressing the pups can lead to orphaned young within your home, which creates a whole new set of problems and ethical concerns. Our technicians are trained to identify nesting sites and take appropriate action when young squirrels are present, prioritizing their safe removal and reunification with the mother whenever possible. However, delaying removal until the young are old enough to be safely relocated is sometimes the most humane approach, although it means tolerating the issue for a period.

Fall and Winter: Seeking Shelter

As temperatures drop and winter approaches, squirrels actively seek warm and sheltered locations to overwinter. Your attic, walls, or chimney can become a very attractive option for them. During the fall, you might observe squirrels frantically gathering and storing food, which can also lead them into conflicts with homeowners, especially if they are burying nuts in your lawn or garden. In the winter months, squirrel activity may decrease as they rely on their stored food and remain hunkered down in their chosen shelters. However, their presence inside your home can still cause significant damage during this period, even if you don't hear as much activity. The cold weather can make removal more challenging as squirrels are less likely to venture outside. Implementing exclusion methods during the colder months is still crucial to prevent future infestations once the squirrels are removed or voluntarily leave in the warmer months. Addressing the issue before the harshest winter weather sets in can also be beneficial, as it prevents them from settling in for the long haul.

Considerations for Removal Timing

The ideal time for squirrel removal often depends on whether young are present. If you suspect squirrels are nesting in your home in the spring or summer, it's wise to contact a professional immediately for assessment. They can determine if there are young and advise on the most appropriate course of action. Sometimes, waiting until the young are old enough to survive on their own before attempting removal is the most humane and effective approach. However, if the infestation is causing immediate damage or posing a health risk, removal may be necessary regardless of the season or the presence of young. We will always prioritize safety and humane practices, working with you to find the best solution for your specific situation and the time of year. It's always best to consult with a professional to weigh the pros and cons of timing your squirrel removal. Don't hesitate to call us to discuss your options.

Preventing Future Squirrel Infestations

Removing squirrels is just one part of the solution; preventing them from returning is equally important. By taking proactive steps to secure your home and make it less attractive to these persistent creatures, you can significantly reduce the risk of future infestations. Kankakee Pest Pros can provide valuable advice and assistance with squirrel prevention measures in Kankakee, IL.

Seal All Entry Points

As mentioned earlier, sealing all potential entry points is the most crucial step in preventing squirrels from entering your home. Conduct a thorough inspection of your roofline, eaves, chimney, vents, siding, and foundation for any gaps, cracks, or holes. Pay close attention to areas where different building materials meet, as these often create small openings. Use durable materials like heavy-gauge wire mesh, metal flashing, or concrete to seal these openings. Ensure that chimney caps are properly installed and in good condition. Repair any damaged roof shingles or siding promptly. Remember, squirrels can squeeze through surprisingly small spaces, so be meticulous in your inspection and sealing efforts. Think about using a strong sealant designed for outdoor use. Don't underestimate their ability to chew through weak materials; invest in robust solutions for long-term effectiveness and consider interlinking to our wildlife control page for more broader prevention tips.

Trim Tree Branches

Trees that overhang your roof or are close to your home's exterior can provide squirrels with easy access. Trim back any branches that are within several feet of your roofline or windows. This creates a physical barrier, making it more difficult for squirrels to jump onto your roof. Regularly maintaining your trees and shrubs can also help eliminate potential hiding spots and reduce their overall presence near your home. Consider the distance carefully; squirrels are surprisingly capable jumpers. Regular tree maintenance is a simple yet effective preventive measure that should not be overlooked.

Secure Food Sources

Squirrels are attracted to readily available food sources. Secure your garbage cans with tight-fitting lids to prevent them from foraging in your trash. If you have bird feeders, consider using squirrel-proof feeders or placing them away from your home. Clean up fallen fruits or nuts from your yard promptly. Store pet food in sealed containers, especially if it's kept outdoors. Eliminating easy access to food makes your property less appealing to squirrels and encourages them to seek sustenance elsewhere. Simple steps like securing garbage can lids can make a significant difference in deterring squirrels and other nuisance animals like raccoons.

Install Barriers Around Gardens

If squirrels are a problem in your garden, consider installing barriers such as netting, cages, or fencing to protect your plants. There are also various commercially available squirrel repellents and deterrents that can be used in gardens, although their effectiveness can vary. Experiment with different methods to find what works best for your specific garden setup and the local squirrel population. Physical barriers are often the most reliable method for protecting vulnerable plants and crops from determined squirrels looking for a free meal.
